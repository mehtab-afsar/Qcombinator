## 1. Executive Summary

**Priority ICP:** UK mid-market manufacturers (50–250 employees) with dedicated operations or procurement leads experiencing procurement inefficiency and supplier fragmentation.

**Secondary ICP:** UK manufacturers (250–500 employees) with established procurement teams seeking cost reduction and process automation.

**Tertiary ICP:** UK contract manufacturers and precision engineering firms with complex multi-supplier ecosystems.

**Highest-Value Industry:** Precision engineering, automotive components, industrial equipment manufacturing.

**Strongest Commercial Opportunity:** Operations Directors and Heads of Procurement in 50–150 employee manufacturers who currently manage procurement manually or via spreadsheets, face supplier fragmentation, and report procurement overhead consuming 15–25% of operational capacity.

**Recommended Commercial Focus:** Prioritise 50–150 employee manufacturers in the Midlands and North of England with operations or procurement leads who have budget authority and are experiencing acute procurement pain (supplier delays, cost overruns, manual RFQ processes).

## 4. Priority ICP Profiles

### **ICP 1: Mid-Market Manufacturer (50–150 Employees)**

**Organisation Description:** UK-based manufacturers producing precision components, industrial equipment, or automotive parts. Typically family-owned or private-equity-backed. Revenue £5m–£25m. Procurement managed by Operations Director or Head of Operations.

**Industry:** Precision engineering, automotive components, industrial equipment, contract manufacturing.

**Geography:** Midlands, North of England, South East (M1/M6 corridor priority).

**Typical Use Cases:** Supplier discovery for custom components; RFQ comparison across 5–15 suppliers; purchase-order approval workflow automation; procurement spend visibility.

**Business Objectives:** Reduce procurement overhead; improve supplier lead times; control cost overruns; free operational capacity for production planning.

**Buying Motivations:** Procurement is a visible operational bottleneck. Manual RFQ processes consume 15–25% of operations team capacity. Supplier delays impact production schedules. No procurement-specific tooling in place.

**Purchasing Characteristics:** Single decision-maker (Operations Director). Budget authority up to £30k annually without board approval. Evaluation cycle 30–60 days. Prefers pilot-to-paid progression. Low tolerance for complex implementation.

**Strategic Importance:** Fastest path to conversion. High reference value. Expansion potential as organisation scales.

**ICP Score:** **9/10**

---

### **ICP 2: Established Mid-Market Manufacturer (150–250 Employees)**

**Organisation Description:** UK manufacturers with dedicated procurement or supply chain teams. Revenue £25m–£75m. Procurement managed by Head of Procurement or Supply Chain Manager reporting to COO or CFO.

**Industry:** Automotive, aerospace components, industrial machinery, electronics manufacturing.

**Geography:** UK-wide, concentration in Midlands, South East, Scotland.

**Typical Use Cases:** Procurement process standardisation; supplier performance tracking; RFQ workflow automation; spend analytics; compliance documentation.

**Business Objectives:** Reduce procurement cost base by 20–30%; improve supplier negotiation leverage; increase procurement team productivity; demonstrate cost savings to board.

**Buying Motivations:** Procurement team exists but lacks modern tooling. Fragmented supplier data. Manual RFQ comparison limits negotiation effectiveness. Board pressure to demonstrate cost control.

**Purchasing Characteristics:** Dual approval (Head of Procurement + CFO or COO). Budget authority £50k–£100k annually. Evaluation cycle 60–90 days. Requires ROI justification and vendor references.

**Strategic Importance:** Higher contract value. Strong reference potential. Expansion into adjacent use cases (supplier risk, compliance).

**ICP Score:** **7/10**

---

### **ICP 3: Contract Manufacturer / Precision Engineering Firm (50–200 Employees)**

**Organisation Description:** UK contract manufacturers or precision engineering firms serving automotive, aerospace, or defence sectors. Complex multi-supplier ecosystems. Revenue £10m–£50m.

**Industry:** Contract manufacturing, precision engineering, CNC machining, tooling.

**Geography:** Midlands, North West, South East.

**Typical Use Cases:** Supplier discovery for specialised materials; RFQ comparison for low-volume high-complexity components; purchase-order tracking; supplier lead-time management.

**Business Objectives:** Reduce supplier lead times; improve material cost predictability; increase supplier diversity; maintain production schedule adherence.

**Buying Motivations:** Supplier fragmentation creates procurement complexity. Custom component sourcing is time-intensive. Production delays caused by supplier lead-time variability. Operations team stretched thin.

**Purchasing Characteristics:** Decision-maker is Operations Director or Managing Director. Budget authority £20k–£50k annually. Evaluation cycle 30–60 days. High urgency during production crises.

**Strategic Importance:** High pain intensity. Fast conversion potential. Strong word-of-mouth within manufacturing networks.

**ICP Score:** **8/10**
